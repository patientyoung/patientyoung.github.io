Project overview

Converted a low‑cost, stock Creality Ender‑3 Pro into a high‑speed, high‑precision rapid‑prototyping system. Goal: push performance limits while maintaining dimensional accuracy, reliability, and repeatability comparable to commercial printers in a higher price bracket.

Engineering objectives

  • Increase maximum stable print speed without sacrificing print quality.
  • Reduce vibration‑induced artifacts through data‑driven motion tuning.
  • Improve system reliability and uptime via wiring + firmware redesign.
  • Expand material capability and volumetric flow performance.
  • Deliver professional‑grade performance using cost‑efficient upgrades.

Constraints

  • Low‑cost base platform (Ender‑3 Pro) with tight budget constraints.
  • Maintain accuracy and reliability at high acceleration / high volumetric flow.
  • Minimize toolhead mass while maximizing cooling and rigidity.
  • Ensure safe electrical design (thermistors, power stability, signal integrity).
  • Target dimensional accuracy: ±[tolerance mm] mm.

Approach

  1. Mechanical integration: Installed BMG dual‑gear extruder, high‑flow hotend, BLTouch probing, and ADXL345 accelerometer; designed custom toolhead mounts/ducts using parametric CAD to optimize mass, airflow, and rigidity.
  2. Firmware + motion control: Migrated to Klipper on a Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W; ran accelerometer‑based resonance testing and applied input shaping; tuned acceleration, pressure advance, and related motion parameters for stability.
  3. Electrical reliability: Built custom JST‑XH wiring harnesses and improved cable routing; diagnosed thermistor faults, firmware issues, and power‑related failures; validated safety and signal integrity with multimeter diagnostics.
  4. Testing + optimization: Iterated with calibration geometry and real parts; compared slicer configs and volumetric flow limits to find bottlenecks; logged config changes for reproducibility/version control.
